**Date:** June 8, 2026

Professionally, I have always kept a steady cadence with certification exams for different reasons: career positioning, proving that I understood the basics of a product or service, avoiding questions when delivering training or consulting, and most importantly, continuously trying to stay up to date.
My public transcript is available here: https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/users/luticm79/transcript/7xomoa8gk3l0kzk

On 05/30/2026, I took the *DP-300: Administering Microsoft Azure SQL Solutions* exam, adding one more certification to my resume: *Microsoft Certified: Azure Database Administrator Associate*.

I chose this exam because I have been getting closer to SQL Server again, with an emphasis on Azure SQL. So, there is nothing better than putting an exam on the calendar to set a clear deadline. My usual approach is to review the exam topics and plan a study routine by going through the learning paths (https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/resources/study-guides/dp-300 and https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/courses/dp-300t00).

Unlike certifications I took in the past, this time I used a different method: I started with the assessments in the Microsoft Learn portal and reviewed the questions I got wrong, as well as questions where the explanation or one of the answer options referenced a setting or topic I wanted to explore further (https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/azure-database-administrator-associate/practice/assessment?assessment-type=practice&assessmentId=58&practice-assessment-type=certification).

Compared to the actual exam, the assessments proved to be very useful, covering the listed topics, question formats, and significantly speeding up my study time. I also used Pearson's practice test (available on O'Reilly) [DP-300 Administering Microsoft Azure SQL Solutions](https://learning.oreilly.com/certifications/9780135448687/), and if I had to choose only one, I would comfortably choose Microsoft Learn's.
There are scenario-based question styles that the assessments do not cover, but I found only a small number of those in the exam (I imagine that is standard for this certification).

I consider this a medium-complexity certification. It does not require candidates to memorize syntax (which is excellent, especially these days), and I believe it fulfills its purpose well: validating whether the candidate has a solid foundation to evaluate different Azure SQL solutions, hybrid scenarios, HA/DR, and security (in my case, many questions were about security).

Reflecting on the experience, it was interesting to notice a very positive feeling: completing a technical activity without any AI assistance. Do not get me wrong, I use AI solutions frequently and I believe we should learn to be increasingly productive, but I do have questions about the impact on our cognitive capabilities (a topic for another post).

If you work with databases and Azure, I hope you get the opportunity to earn this certification.
